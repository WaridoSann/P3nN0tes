An attack in cybersecurity isn't just a random event with no logic or reflection upstream; it is something precise that hackers know. Let's look deeply into what the lifecycle of an attack is!

Consider this: an attack is a logical scheme with these steps: Source, Processes, Privileges, and Destination. The source is the thing triggered, like when we inject malicious code or a library into a user-input. Processes are the moment where the information is interpreted as expected by the hacker; processes involve PIDs and accounting, where our information can be executed by our victim, for example.

Privileges are what our attack is limited or authorized to touch (SYSTEM/root, User). If the attacked service runs with root permissions, the attack could be very efficient. Destination is the moment where the attack stops, like getting a reverse shell or an SSH connection established.

These were the four pillars of the concept of an attack.
