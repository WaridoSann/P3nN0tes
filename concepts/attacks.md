An Attack in cybersécurity isnt' just things with no logic or reflexion upstream, that's something precise that hackers knows, let's see deeply what is the lifecycle of an attack ! 

Consider this : An attack is a logical scheme with these steps, Source, Processes, Privileges and Destination. 
The source is the thing triggered like we inject a malicious code or library in a user-input, processes are the moment where the information is interpreted as expected by the hacker, processes are PIDs and Accounting our information can be executed by our victim for example.
Privileges are what our attack is limited or authorized to touch (SYSTEM/root, User) if the attacked service run as root permissions the attack could be very efficient.
Destination, that's the moment where the attack stops like got a reverse shell or a ssh connection established.

These were the four pillars of the concept of a "Attack".
